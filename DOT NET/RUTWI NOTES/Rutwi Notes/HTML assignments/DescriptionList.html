Rose 
A rose is either a woody perennial flowering plant of the genus Rosa, in the family Rosaceae, or the flower it bears. 
			          There are over three hundred species and tens of thousands of cultivars.[citation needed] They form a group of plants 
					  that can be erect shrubs, climbing, or trailing, with stems that are often armed with sharp prickles.

Jasmine 
 Jasmine is a genus of shrubs and vines in the olive family. It contains around 200 species native to tropical and warm temperate 
			     regions of Eurasia, Africa, and Oceania. Jasmines are widely cultivated for the characteristic fragrance of their flowers. 
				 A number of unrelated plants contain the word "jasmine" in their common names.
